  • Understanding Company Incorporation

    Company incorporation is the process of legally establishing a business entity under the Companies Act, 2013. It involves various steps, including obtaining Digital Signature Certificates (DSCs), Director Identification Numbers (DINs), and filing the necessary documents with the Registrar of Companies (ROC).

  • Types of Companies

    There are several types of companies that can be incorporated in India, each with its own set of advantages and requirements:

    1. Private Limited Company: Suitable for small to medium-sized businesses, offering limited liability protection and ease of ownership transfer.
    2. Public Limited Company: Ideal for large businesses looking to raise capital from the public, with stricter regulatory requirements.
    3. One Person Company (OPC): Allows a single individual to incorporate a company, providing limited liability protection.
    4. Limited Liability Partnership (LLP): A hybrid form of business entity that combines the features of a partnership and a company, offering limited liability to its partners.
    5. Nidhi Company: A type of company that belongs to the non-banking finance sector, recognized under section 406 of the Companies Act, 2013, and is formed to borrow and lend money to its members.
    6. Section 8 Company: A company registered under section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, for promoting commerce, art, science, sports, education, research, social welfare, religion, charity, protection of environment, or any other charitable object.
    7. Micro-Finance Company: A company that provides financial services to low-income individuals or groups who typically lack access to banking and related services.

    The following documents are required for company incorporation:

    1. Identity and address proof of promoters/directors/shareholders.
    2. PAN card of promoters/directors/shareholders
    3. Proof of registered office address i.e. Electricity Bill and or Rent Agreement.
    4. MOA and AOA.
